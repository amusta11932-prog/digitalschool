What is AI?

Artificial Intelligence, or AI, is like a computer program that can learn and make decisions — kind of like how your brain learns from experience. Instead of being told exactly what to do for every situation, AI figures things out by looking at lots of examples.

Think of it like how you learned to recognize cats. Nobody gave you a checklist of "pointy ears + whiskers + tail = cat." You just saw lots of cats, and your brain figured out the pattern. AI does the same thing, but with math and massive amounts of data.

1

Data goes in

We feed the AI tons of examples — images, text, numbers, anything.

2

Model finds patterns

The AI analyzes the data and discovers connections humans might miss.

3

Prediction comes out

Given new input, the AI uses what it learned to make a smart guess.

AI in Your Daily Life

📺

YouTube Recommendations

YouTube's AI watches what you watch, like, and skip to learn your preferences. Then it suggests new videos it thinks you'll enjoy next.

📱

Face Unlock

Your phone's AI has studied thousands of facial images to recognize what makes your face unique. It compares your face to this stored pattern to unlock your phone safely.

🦉

Duolingo Adapts

Duolingo's AI tracks which words you get right, wrong, and how long you take to answer. Based on this, it adjusts the difficulty and focuses on areas where you need more practice.
